Module 5 — Chapter 3

Emotional Contagion

“Before words are processed, states are already transmitted.”

5.3.1 Emotions Are Not Private

Modern culture treats emotions as internal, personal experiences.

This is incorrect.

Emotions are energetic states that broadcast continuously through posture, tone, micro-expression, breathing, and pacing.

5.3.2 The Nervous System Network

Humans are not isolated nervous systems.

We are nodes in a biological network that constantly syncs and re-syncs.

This is why anxiety spreads in rooms — and calm can dissolve it.

5.3.3 Why Words Rarely Change Atmosphere

People attempt to calm situations with explanations.

But explanation cannot override physiology.

A dysregulated body cannot be convinced by logic.

5.3.4 Emotional Authority

The person whose emotional state remains coherent becomes the emotional authority — whether they intend to or not.

Others unconsciously entrain to them.

Exercise — Contagion Awareness

  • Enter a room and notice its emotional tone.
  • Do not speak for two minutes.
  • Stabilize your breathing and posture.
  • Observe how the room responds.

5.3.5 Why Chaos Feels Magnetic

Highly emotional states are loud.

They pull attention because they are unresolved.

Upstream work teaches you to resist being pulled into turbulence.

5.3.6 Emotional Hygiene

Just as bodies require cleanliness, emotional systems require hygiene.

Without it, you absorb states that are not yours.

Practice — Boundary Without Walls

  • Notice when another person’s emotion enters your body.
  • Do not resist it.
  • Do not amplify it.
  • Return to breath and posture.

5.3.7 Journaling Prompts

  • Which emotions do I absorb most easily?
  • What states do I transmit unconsciously?
  • How does my body signal calm or stress?
  • What changes when I regulate first?
